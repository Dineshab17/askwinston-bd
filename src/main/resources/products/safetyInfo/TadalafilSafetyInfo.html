USES

Tadalafil is the active ingredient in Cialis and therefore the generic version of Cialis. It is used to help manage and treat sexual dysfunction, specifically erectile dysfunction. It works by relaxing blood vessels and allowing for increased blood flow to the penis in combination with sexual arousal and stimulation.

Tadalafil is also available to treat high blood pressure a condition known as pulmonary hypertension as well as symptoms caused by an enlarged prostate known as benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH). Do not take this medication if you are taking any other medications for erectile dysfunction or pulmonary hypertension including drugs belonging to the same class/group as sildenafil (i.e. Tadalafil, Vardenafil).

HOW TO USE

Read and review the medication information sheet provided by your pharmacist before you start taking Tadalafil.

To treat erectile dysfunction, there are 2 ways that tadalafil may be prescribed and this may often be based on your frequency of sexual activity. If you are taking Tadalafil on an as needed basis you will likely be prescribed the 20 mg pill to be taken every 36 hours as needed, usually at least 30 minutes before sexual activity. Tadalafil's effect on sexual ability may last up to 36 hours. The second way to treat ED is to take Tadalafil regularly, once a day every day with a 5 mg once daily pill. If you take it this way, you may attempt sexual activity at any time between your doses. Be sure to tell your doctor and pharmacist about all the products you use (including prescription drugs, non-prescription drugs, and herbal products).

Do not share this medication with others.

SIDE EFFECTS

The most common side effects that may be experienced with Tadalafil include dizziness, nausea, upset stomach, headaches, facial flushing, back pain and muscle aches/pain. Additionally, visual changes may be experienced (i.e. temporary difficulty with colour differentiation, light sensitivity, blurry vision). If any of these symptoms persist or worsen after taking Tadalafil seek out medical care as soon as possible.

Uncommon yet serious side effects requiring immediate medical attention include severe dizziness, fainting, chest/jaw/left arm pain, sudden decrease or loss of hearing, a painful or prolonged erection greater than 4 hours, sudden decreased vision, including permanent blindness, in one or both eyes (NAION), You have a slightly higher risk of developing NAION if you have heart disease, diabetes, high cholesterol, high blood pressure, if you are over 50, or if you smoke.

As with any drug, an allergic reaction may be possible. Symptoms of an allergic reaction include a rash, itching/swelling, especially of the face/tongue/throat, severe dizziness, trouble breathing. If you notice any of the above symptoms while taking this medication discontinue the medication and seek out medical care immediately. There may be additional side effects with Tadalafil. If you notice other effects not listed above, contact your doctor or pharmacist. You may also report side effects to Health Canada at 1-866-234-2345.

PRECAUTIONS

Before taking Tadalafil, tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are allergic to it; or if you have any other allergies. This product may contain inactive ingredients, which can cause allergic reactions or other problems. Talk to your pharmacist for more details regarding the medication if you have a history of reactions to certain ingredients, fillers, dyes.

Before using this medication, tell your doctor or pharmacist your medical history:

  • Heart problems (such as heart attack or life-threatening irregular heartbeat in the past 6 months, chest pain/angina, heart failure).
  • Stroke in the past 6 months.
  • Kidney disease.
  • Liver disease.
  • High or low blood pressure.
  • Dehydration.
  • Conditions of the penis ie angulation, fibrosis/scarring, peyronie's disease.
  • History of painful/prolonged erection (priapism).
  • Sickle cell anemia.
  • Leukemia.
  • Multiple myeloma.
  • Eye problems (such as retinitis pigmentosa, sudden decreased vision, NAION).

This drug may make you dizzy or cause vision problems. Alcohol or marijuana (cannabis) can make you dizzier. Do not drive, use machinery, or do anything that requires alertness or clear vision until you can do it safely. Limit alcoholic beverages. Talk to your doctor if you are using marijuana (cannabis). Tadalafil does not protect against sexually transmitted diseases. Practice "safe sex" such as using condoms.

DRUG INTERACTIONS

Drug interactions may change how your medications work or increase your risk for serious side effects. Keep a list of all the products you use (including prescription/non-prescription drugs and herbal products) and share it with your doctor and pharmacist. Do not start, stop, or change the dosage of any medicines without your doctor's approval. A product that may interact with this drug is: riociguat. Tadalafil can cause a serious drop in your blood pressure when used with nitrates. A serious drop in blood pressure can lead to dizziness, fainting, and rarely heart attack or stroke.

Do not use Tadalafil with any of the following:

  • Nitrates such as nitroglycerin, isosorbide recreational drugs called "poppers" containing amyl nitrate, amyl nitrite, or butyl nitrite.
  • Alpha blocker medication (such as doxazosin, tamsulosin) you may start treatment with a lower dose of sildenafil to minimize your risk of low blood pressure.
  • Other medications can affect how Tadalafil metabolizes in your body, which may affect how sildenafil works include:
    • Azole antifungals (such as itraconazole, ketoconazole).
    • Macrolide antibiotics (such as clarithromycin, erythromycin).
    • HIV protease inhibitors (such as ritonavir, saquinavir).
    • Hepatitis C virus protease inhibitors (such as boceprevir, telaprevir).
    • Mifepristone.
    • Rifampin.

Do not take this medication with any other product that contains Tadalafil or other similar medications for erectile dysfunction or pulmonary hypertension (such as Sildenafil, Vardenafil).

MISSED DOSE

If you are taking Tadalafil on a regular schedule and you forget to take a dose, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it is almost time for your next dose, skip the missed dose and go back to your regular dosing schedule. Do not take two doses to make up for the dose you missed.

OVERDOSE

If someone has overdosed and has serious symptoms such as loss of consciousness, trouble breathing, chest pain call 911. Otherwise, call your provincial poison control center right away.

STORAGE

Store at room temperature away from light and moisture. Keep all medications away from children and pets. Do not flush medications down the toilet or pour them into a drain unless instructed to do so. Properly discard this product when it is expired or no longer needed. Consult your pharmacist or local waste disposal company.
